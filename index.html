Project Topic - Government Revenue

Keywords

Government Revenue

Government revenue is the total income earned by the government from all source to fund its activities and development programs.

It mainly includes tax revenue (direct & indirect taxes) and non tax revenue (fees, fines, dividends, interest, etc.)

Revenue Receipts

Revenue receipts are the regular incomes of the government which do not create any liability or reduce assets.

They include tax revenue (like income tax, GST) and non tax revenue (like fees, fines, interest, dividends).

Capital Receipts

Capital receipts are the funds received by the government that either create a liability or reduce its assets.

They include borrowings (like loans from RBI , World Bank), disinvestment proceeds, and recovery of loans. Unlike revenue receipts, they are not regular in nature & are mainly used to meet long terms developmental and investment needs of the country.

Tax Revenue

Tax revenue is the income collected by the government through various taxes imposed on individuals & businesses.

It includes direct taxes (like income tax, corporate tax) and indirect taxes (like GST, custom duty).

Non-Tax Revenue

Non tax revenue is the income that the government earns from sources other than taxes.

It includes fees, fines, penalties, interest, dividends from public sector enterprises, profits from the Reserve Bank of India & revenue from services provided.

Direct Tax

Direct tax is the tax that individuals or organisations pay directly to the government based on income or wealth.

Examples: Income Tax, Corporate Tax, Wealth Tax.

Indirect Tax

Indirect tax is a type that is collected by intermediaries (like shops or businesses) from consumer on the sale of goods & services.

Examples: GST, Customs Duty, Excise Duty.
